Place the ball on the pitch.

Deep breaths.

Eyes ahead.

This kick will win the game.

So you better check your aim.

Free kicks require a player to aim at one thing—the goal.

Players practice free kicks because even though they are “free”, they are often the difference between winning and losing.

Colossians 3:23-24: “Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters, since you know that you will receive an inheritance from the Lord as a reward. It is the Lord Christ you are serving.”

Free kicks and faith are both gifts.

But they are not gifts to be taken lightly.

They require hard work and a singular aim.

Faith has one aim—to know Jesus and walk with Him daily.

It’s a journey that requires your whole heart and an eternal focus.

Faith in Jesus is the center—the aim.

And because of this, our goal in life becomes clear.

Faith means there’s more to your story. What’s your aim?
