What happens on the football field gives cause for many celebrations.

The amazing save.

The perfect pass.

The bicycle kick shot that whooshes past the keeper and wins the game.

The biggest cause for celebration? When the Jules Rimet trophy is lifted high by the conquering team.

The Bible too speaks of a prize…a crown to wear for eternity.

1 Corinthians 9:24-25: “Do you not know that in a race all the runners run, but only one gets the prize? Run in such a way as to get the prize. Everyone who competes in the games goes into strict training. They do it to get a crown that will not last, but we do it to get a crown that will last forever.”

What will make us win this crown that lasts forever?

Is it doing good things?

Is it being a moral person?

Or is it something more?

No matter how good we may be, it is not enough to earn the prize.

But that is where Jesus comes in.

Celebration—What is this prize that lasts forever?
